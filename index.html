About Us

We have developed an innovative product for duplicating movements from a tracer to a copier which has wide application in the cognitive development of young children.

We are looking to develop this product further and reach out to the intended customer.

  • Our Objective
  • Our Goals
  • Our Product

Children have an instinctive urge to recreate pictures, shapes, patterns way see around themselves in books, nature, television, internet etc., This urge is an expression of the innate creativity lying dormant in every child.

The drawing plays a big role in the cognitive development of a child. It helps the child to think creatively, develop hand-eye coordination, hone analytic skills and conceptualise ideas.

To enhance and bring out artistic talent in children without the boredom of Conventional/usual methods of drawing and give an experiential dimension to the monotonous activity of just copying.

Design work to be taken up - leading to a final, marketable, aesthetically suitable product.

Marketing survey has to be carried out to assess its selling potential.

Manufacturing facilities to meet quality standards, production demand, inventory, shipping assignments etc., have to be studied.

Company formation, registration formalities, setting up of administration, infrastructure and compliance to Government norms and obligations etc., are to be set.

‘YOUNG ARTIST’ is an innovative product for duplicating movements from a tracer to a copier.

By experiential learning we mean not just copying but to engage the children's mind to quickly grasp the concepts/ideas of physical dimensions and proportionalities, scaling, laying out/spacing of drawing in a limited size of paper, comparing original and replica made, perfecting strokes of drawing besides interestingly involved in a mechanism during drawing.

Our process of drawing encompasses of all the advantages of conventional drawing methods such as Dot method, Symmetric method, Oil applied paper method, , Grid methods, Carbon paper copying etc.,

YOUNG ARTIST

We have developed a device ‘YOUNG ARTIST’ to fine tune artistic skills of children without the boredom of conventional methods of drawing and entails drawing activity an experiential learning.

Handling the device is easy by children.

Drawing is physically seen while copying unlike in a carbon paper the tracing is not visible as the paper is kept underneath the original drawing.

Over stroking/repeating of strokes can be avoided.
